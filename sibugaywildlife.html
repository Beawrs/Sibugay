Overview

Wildlife tourism in Zamboanga Sibugay offers a rare glimpse into untouched ecosystems. From the migratory bird flyways over our wetlands to the vibrant coral reefs beneath the Sibugay Bay, the province is a haven for nature enthusiasts and conservationists alike.

Our wildlife initiatives focus on protection and education, allowing visitors to witness the beauty of rare species while supporting the preservation of their natural habitats.

Popular Wildlife Destinations

Sibugay Wetland Nature Reserve

Sibugay Wetland Nature Reserve is an important eco-tourism site in Zamboanga Sibugay known for its rich biodiversity and natural wetlands ecosystem. It serves as a habitat for various bird species, fish, and other wildlife, making it ideal for birdwatching and nature exploration.

Buluan Island Marine Sanctuary

Located off the coast of Ipil, Buluan Island is home to a protected marine sanctuary. The crystal-clear waters house diverse coral formations and a huge variety of tropical fish, offering world-class snorkeling opportunities for those looking to explore underwater wildlife.

Kabug Mangrove Park and Wetlands

Kabug Mangrove Park and Wetlands is one of the most important eco-cultural attractions in Zamboanga Sibugay, located in the municipality of Siay. It features a wide area of preserved mangrove forests that serve as a natural protection against coastal erosion and storms. The park has wooden boardwalks that allow visitors to walk through the mangroves while observing the rich biodiversity, including fish, crabs, and various bird species.
